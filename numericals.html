01 KNN easy
Classify P=(5,5) with K=1. Training: A(2,3)=Red, B(6,6)=Blue, C(7,4)=Red, D(1,1)=Blue. Use Euclidean distance.
✅ check my answer
d(A)=√[(5−2)²+(5−3)²]=√13=3.61
d(B)=√[1+1]=√2=1.41
d(C)=√[4+1]=√5=2.24
d(D)=√[16+16]=√32=5.66
Nearest = B (1.41) → Blue.
02 KNN medium

✅ check my answer

K=3: B(Blue), C(Red), A(Red) → Red=2, Blue=1 → Red.
Note how K=1 said Blue but K=3 says Red — K matters!

08 Gradient Descent easy
w=2, ∂J/∂w=1.5, α=0.1. Find new w after one step.
✅ check my answer

09 Gradient Descent medium
Data x=[1,2], y=[1,2]. Start w=0, b=0, α=0.1. Do ONE full iteration (find new w and b).
✅ check my answer
Predictions: 0,0. Errors: 0−1=−1, 0−2=−2.
∂J/∂w=(1/2)[(−1)(1)+(−2)(2)]=(1/2)(−5)=−2.5
∂J/∂b=(1/2)(−1−2)=−1.5

15 Naive Bayes easy
Why does a likelihood of 0 (e.g. P(Long|Mango)=0) ruin a Naive Bayes score, and what fixes it?
✅ check my answer
Because the class score is a PRODUCT — multiplying by 0 makes the whole score 0, no matter how good the other features are. Fix: Laplace smoothing — add 1 to every count so nothing is ever exactly 0.
16 Entropy easy
Compute the entropy of a group with 9 Yes and 5 No (14 total). (log₂(9/14)≈−0.637, log₂(5/14)≈−1.485)
✅ check my answer
H = −(9/14)(−0.637) − (5/14)(−1.485) = 0.410 + 0.530 = 0.940.
17 Entropy easy
A group has 5 Yes, 5 No. What is its entropy? What does the value tell you?
✅ check my answer
H = −(0.5)log₂(0.5) − (0.5)log₂(0.5) = 0.5 + 0.5 = 1.0. Maximum entropy = perfectly mixed = the most confused a binary group can be.

23 PCA hard
Continuing: covariance = (1/2)ZZᵀ. Compute the covariance matrix and its eigenvalues.
✅ check my answer
ZZᵀ[0,0]=4+0+4=8, [0,1]=8+0+8=16, [1,1]=16+0+16=32.
Cov=(1/2)[[8,16],[16,32]]=[[4,8],[8,16]].
det(Cov−λI)=0 → (4−λ)(16−λ)−64=0 → λ²−20λ=0 → λ₁=20, λ₂=0.
PC1 holds 100% of variance; drop PC2.
